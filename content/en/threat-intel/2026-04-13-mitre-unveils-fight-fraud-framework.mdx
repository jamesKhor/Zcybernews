## Executive Summary

MITRE has released the Fight Fraud Framework, a comprehensive model that outlines the tactics, techniques, and procedures (TTPs) used by fraudsters. This framework provides a structured approach to understanding and mitigating fraud across various industries.

## Technical Analysis

The Fight Fraud Framework is designed to be a behavior-based model, focusing on the actions and methods employed by fraudsters rather than specific tools or indicators. This approach allows organizations to identify and counteract fraudulent activities more effectively. The framework is composed of several key components, including tactics, techniques, and procedures, each of which is defined and categorized to provide a clear understanding of fraudulent behavior.

According to MITRE, the framework is intended to be flexible and adaptable, accommodating the evolving nature of fraud tactics. It includes a detailed taxonomy of fraud techniques, such as account takeover, identity theft, and payment fraud, among others. Each technique is described in terms of its methodology, potential impacts, and mitigation strategies.

## Tactics, Techniques & Procedures

The Fight Fraud Framework categorizes fraud TTPs into several tactics, including 'Initial Access,' 'Execution,' 'Persistence,' 'Privilege Escalation,' 'Defense Evasion,' 'Credential Access,' 'Discovery,' 'Lateral Movement,' 'Collection,' 'Exfiltration,' and 'Command and Control.' Each tactic is further broken down into specific techniques and procedures that fraudsters employ to achieve their goals.

For example, under the 'Initial Access' tactic, techniques such as phishing and social engineering are detailed. The framework provides a granular view of how these techniques are executed, including the use of malicious links, attachments, and social manipulation to gain unauthorized access to systems or data.

## Threat Actor Context

While the framework does not specifically attribute fraud activities to known threat actors, it provides a comprehensive overview of the methods used by various fraudsters. This context allows security professionals to map observed fraudulent activities to the tactics and techniques outlined in the framework, aiding in the identification and attribution of threat actors.

## Detection & Hunting Queries

The Fight Fraud Framework suggests several detection and hunting queries that organizations can use to identify fraudulent activities. These queries are designed to be integrated into existing security information and event management (SIEM) systems and other security tools. Examples include queries for detecting unusual login patterns, unauthorized access attempts, and anomalous data exfiltration activities.

## Mitigations & Recommendations

MITRE recommends a multi-layered approach to mitigating fraud risks. This includes implementing robust identity and access management (IAM) controls, enhancing user awareness and training, and deploying advanced threat detection and response solutions. Additionally, the framework emphasizes the importance of regular security assessments and incident response planning to address potential fraud incidents effectively.
