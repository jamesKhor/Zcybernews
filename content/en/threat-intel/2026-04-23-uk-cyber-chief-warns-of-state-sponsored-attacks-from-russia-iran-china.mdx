## Executive Summary
The chief executive of the UK's National Cyber Security Centre (NCSC), Felicity Oswald, has identified Russia, Iran, and China as the sources of the most serious cyber threats facing the United Kingdom. Oswald warned that British businesses must bolster their defenses, as the UK could be targeted "at scale" if drawn into an international conflict.

## Threat Actor Context
The public attribution points to state-sponsored or state-aligned threat actors from Russia, Iran, and China. These groups are typically associated with advanced persistent threat (APT) campaigns targeting government, critical infrastructure, and private sector organizations for espionage, disruption, or financial gain. The NCSC's statement frames these threats as persistent and geopolitically motivated.

## Mitigations & Recommendations
The NCSC's primary recommendation is for UK businesses to proactively prepare their cyber defenses. This preparation is emphasized as critical for resilience in the event the UK becomes involved in an international conflict that triggers large-scale cyber operations. Organizations are directed to follow NCSC guidance for improving baseline security and incident response readiness.
