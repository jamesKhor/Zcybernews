## Executive Summary

A new analysis from cyber insurance provider Resilience gives chief information security officers (CISOs) concrete financial data to argue for security investments, according to a report published April 28, 2026 by SecurityWeek. The data links specific security control gaps to measurable loss amounts, providing a quantitative basis for budget requests that boards — often more responsive to financial metrics than technical warnings — may find compelling.

## Technical Analysis

Resilience's analysis draws on its underwriting and claims data to correlate the presence or absence of specific security controls with claim frequency and severity. The company did not publicly release the full dataset or methodology, but the core finding is that organizations with documented gaps in controls such as multi-factor authentication (MFA), endpoint detection and response (EDR), and backup integrity testing experienced higher average losses per incident. The report frames these gaps not as abstract risk but as direct financial exposure, enabling CISOs to present a cost-benefit case for remediation.

SecurityWeek notes that the analysis is part of a broader trend where cyber insurers are sharing anonymized claims data to help policyholders improve their security posture. This approach contrasts with traditional cyber insurance underwriting, which often relies on questionnaires and external scans rather than real-world loss data. The Resilience report is positioned as a tool for internal advocacy rather than a public benchmark.

## Mitigations & Recommendations

CISOs should consider using the Resilience data — or similar analyses from their own insurers — to build a financial case for specific security improvements. The report suggests that controls with the strongest correlation to reduced losses include enforcing MFA across all external-facing services, deploying EDR with 24/7 monitoring, and regularly testing backup restoration processes. Defenders should request from their insurers any available claims data that maps to their own control environment, as the specificity of the data may vary by provider.
