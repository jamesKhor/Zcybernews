## Executive Summary
Most enterprise deployments of artificial intelligence for cybersecurity stall after the initial demonstration phase, failing to transition into sustained operational use. According to an analysis by The Hacker News, the failure is rarely due to flawed core technology but is instead caused by operational hurdles including poor data quality, complex integration requirements, and unexpected costs. This stall prevents organizations from realizing promised gains in threat detection and automated response.

## Technical Analysis
The primary technical barrier identified is the mismatch between the clean, curated data used in AI demos and the messy, unstructured, and siloed data typical of enterprise production environments. AI models trained on idealized datasets frequently degrade in performance when exposed to real-world log data, which often contains inconsistencies, gaps, and proprietary formats not present in training. Furthermore, integrating AI tools into existing Security Information and Event Management (SIEM) systems, ticketing platforms, and analyst workflows requires significant custom engineering that is seldom accounted for in pre-sale evaluations. The computational cost of running large models continuously on streaming data also creates unforeseen infrastructure burdens, leading to performance bottlenecks or prohibitive cloud bills that were not apparent during controlled demonstrations.

## Mitigations & Recommendations
The source material implies, but does not explicitly state, mitigation paths. Based on the described failure modes, organizations should prioritize data pipeline hygiene and integration testing before purchasing AI security tools. Proof-of-concept evaluations must be conducted using a representative sample of the organization's own live, unfiltered data over a sustained period, not vendor-provided datasets. Teams should also calculate total cost of ownership, including inference compute, retraining cycles, and dedicated engineering support for API integration and maintenance.
