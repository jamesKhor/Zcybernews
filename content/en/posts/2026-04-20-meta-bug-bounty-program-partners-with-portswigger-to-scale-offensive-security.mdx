## Executive Summary
Meta has entered a strategic partnership with PortSwigger, the developer of the Burp Suite web vulnerability scanner, to directly support and scale its global bug bounty researcher community. The initiative will provide select researchers with commercial Burp Suite licenses and targeted training resources. According to a joint announcement, the goal is to enhance the quality and volume of security vulnerabilities reported to Meta’s bug bounty program by improving researcher tooling and education.

## Technical Analysis
The partnership is an operational collaboration rather than a response to a specific vulnerability. PortSwigger will grant commercial Burp Suite licenses to vetted participants in Meta’s bug bounty program. Burp Suite is a de facto standard tool for manual web application security testing, used for tasks like intercepting proxy traffic, automating scans, and manipulating application requests. The arrangement suggests Meta is investing in the offensive security capability of its external researcher pool by removing a primary tooling cost barrier. The announcement did not disclose the number of licenses to be allocated or the specific selection criteria for researchers. The training component is described as focused on “advanced vulnerability discovery techniques,” though the exact curriculum or delivery method was not detailed in the available source material.

## Threat Actor Context
This is not a threat actor disclosure. The article concerns a defensive and collaborative industry initiative between a technology platform (Meta) and a security tool vendor (PortSwigger) to strengthen defensive postures through improved external testing.

## Mitigations & Recommendations
The partnership itself represents a mitigation strategy by Meta to proactively discover and remediate vulnerabilities in its products. For other organizations, the model demonstrates the potential value of investing in the tooling and skill development of external security researchers, whether through formal bug bounty programs or other collaborative channels. Direct, actionable technical mitigations are not the subject of this announcement.
