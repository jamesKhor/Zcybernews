## Executive Summary
Google has officially launched End-to-End Encryption (E2EE) for the Gmail mobile application on Android and iOS, enabling organizations to encrypt email content client-side before it leaves the device. This update addresses growing regulatory demands for data sovereignty and confidentiality in regulated industries.

## Technical Analysis
The new E2EE feature encrypts email content using client-side keys, ensuring that only the sender and intended recipients can decrypt messages. Google has integrated this functionality directly into the Gmail mobile app, removing the need for third-party encryption tools. The implementation leverages the existing Gmail infrastructure while adding an additional encryption layer that operates before data is transmitted to Google's servers.

IT administrators should review Google's documentation for implementation guidelines and consider conducting training sessions to familiarize users with the new encryption workflow.
